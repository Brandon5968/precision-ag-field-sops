# :material-book-open-page-variant: Introduction

What to do when a piece of equipment fails in the field. This manual is for Precision Ag
field techs.

!!! warning "DRAFT — pending sign-off"
    Every repair in this book is `[CONFIRM]` until verified in review. Do not use it in
    the field yet.

!!! info "AT A GLANCE"
    Three outcomes for any fault: **FIELD FIX** (do it here), **HOME FIX** (take it back),
    or **STOP & CALL** (don't touch it). The kit you carry decides which.

## The one decision: field fix, home fix, or stop & call

When something breaks, don't start pulling it apart. First decide which of the three it is.

!!! tip "FIELD FIX"
    You can safely fix it on site **and** the part/tool is in your kit. Complete the SWMS,
    then follow the card.

!!! warning "HOME FIX"
    The fix needs a part or tool you are not carrying, or a workshop. Make the gear safe,
    note what failed, and bring it back. **If it isn't in your kit (Chapter 01), it's a
    home fix.**

!!! danger "STOP & CALL"
    There is a safety risk — fuel, electrical, spring tension, or a raised load — or a
    fault you must report before touching. Stop and call first.

## How to read a repair card

Every fault in this book is written as a **repair card** with the same shape, so you can
scan it fast:

1. **Symptom** — what you see or hear.
2. **Fix type** — a coloured badge: FIELD FIX, HOME FIX, or STOP & CALL.
3. **Tools & parts** — what you need, ticked against your [field kit](01-field-kit.md).
4. **SWMS** — the safe-work steps. Always complete before you start.
5. **Steps** — the procedure, in order.
6. **If this doesn't work** — who to call (see [Support](07-support.md)).

Anything written as `[CONFIRM: …]` is not finalised. Do not guess — it will be confirmed
in the review meeting with the people who do the job.

!!! danger "SWMS"
    Mechanical work carries real hazards — fuel, 12V electrical, moving parts, and spring
    tension. Complete the SWMS for the task before you start. `[CONFIRM: attach official
    SWMS pack; on-site SWMS form (MS Form) planned.]`
